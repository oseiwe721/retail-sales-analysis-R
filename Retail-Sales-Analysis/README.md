# Retail Sales Analysis (R Project)

## Project Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data to uncover key business insights around revenue, profit, customer behavior, product performance, and regional trends.

The goal is to simulate real-world business analysis using R and demonstrate data analysis, visualization, and business thinking skills.

---

## Business Problem

A retail company wants to understand:

- What drives revenue and profit?
- Which products and customers generate the most value?
- Where are losses coming from?
- How performance changes over time?

This analysis helps management make data-driven decisions to improve profitability and growth.

---

## Tools & Technologies

- R
- RStudio
- tidyverse
- janitor
- ggplot2

---

## Dataset

- Source: Superstore Sales Dataset
- Type: Retail transactional data
- Key fields:
  - Sales
  - Profit
  - Customer Name
  - Product Name
  - Category
  - Region
  - Order Date

---

## Key Analysis Performed

### 1. Business KPIs
- Total Revenue
- Total Profit
- Average Sales per Order
- Highest and Lowest Order Value

---

### 2. Customer Analysis
- Top 10 customers by total sales
- Customer contribution to revenue

---

### 3. Product Analysis
- Top-performing products by sales
- Most profitable products
- Loss-making products

---

### 4. Category Performance
- Sales by category
- Profit by category
- Profit margin comparison

---

### 5. Regional Analysis
- Sales performance by region
- Profit distribution across regions

---

### 6. Time Series Analysis
- Monthly sales trends
- Monthly profit trends
- Month-over-month growth rate

---

### 7. Advanced Business Insights
- Profit margin analysis
- Loss impact evaluation
- Product contribution (80/20 rule / Pareto analysis)

---

## Key Insights (Example Findings)

- A small group of products contributes the majority of revenue.
- Some high-sales products are actually loss-making.
- Certain regions generate high sales but lower profit margins.
- Sales performance fluctuates significantly over time.

## Business Recommendations

- Focus marketing on high-profit products, not just high-sales products.
- Reduce or optimize loss-making products.
- Improve performance in low-margin regions.
- Investigate seasonal trends to optimize inventory and marketing.
